Malcolm X

Muslim American Human Rights Activist

"A man who stands for nothing will fall for anything."

Timeline of Malcolm's life

  • 1925 - Born as Malcolm Little in Omaha, Nebraska.
  • 1927 - Malcolm's brother, Reginald is born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
  • 1931 - Malcolm's father, Earl Little, is killed by street car.
  • 1939 - Malcolm's mother, Louise Little, is placed in a State Mental Hospital.
  • 1940 - Lives in various foster homes.
  • 1941 - Moves to Boston and gets exposed to the criminal world."
  • 1943 - Moves to Harlem and gets involved with criminal activites. He also gets the nickname "Detriot Red".
  • 1946 - Gets convicted of larceny, carrying a weapon, and breaking and entering. He gets sentenced to 8 to 10 years in prison. This is where he begins educating himself by reading books.
  • 1947 - Influenced by some family members, letters from Elijah Muhammad (leader of the Nation of Islam), and fellow inmates, Malcolm converts to the followings of the Nation Of Islam. Which sets it's self apart from mainstream Islam by emphasising black nationalism and black pride.
  • 1952 - Malcolm is released from prison after spending 6 years there. He then meets up with Elijah Muhammad in Chicago and is given the infamous "X" in his name.
  • 1953 - The FBI begins spying and keeping a file on Malcolm X as he keeps climbing in position within the Nation of Islam.
  • 1959 - Travels around the world as an ambasssador for the Nation of Islam.
  • 1963 - Criticizes the March on Washington and veiws of intergration with White Americans.
  • 1964 - This year was a life changing and eventful year for Malcolm. He meets with boxer Cassius Clay, who later became known as Muhammad Ali(Malcolm X played a huge role in converting Ali over to the Nation of Islam.) He also met with Martin Luther King Jr. for the first time. This was also the year Malcolm himself left the Nation of Islam and converted to Mainstream Sunni Islam. He then traveled to Mecca to perform the Islamic pilgrimage called "Hajj". There he saw the diversity of muslims and had a renewed spiritual awakening. He would come back to the U.S with a different perspective of white people.
  • 1965 - Malcolm X gets assassinated by radical members of the Nation of Islam.
